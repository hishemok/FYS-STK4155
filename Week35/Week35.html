Exercise 1: Analytical exercises

Show that $$\frac{ \mathbf{b}^T \mathbf{a}}{\partial\mathbf{a}} = \mathbf{b}$$

$$\frac{\partial\mathbf{b}^T\mathbf{a}}{\partial\mathbf{a}} = \frac{\partial(b_0a_0 + b_1a_1 + ... + b_na_n)}{\partial\mathbf{a}} =\begin{bmatrix} \frac{ (b_0a_0 + b_1a_1 + ... + b_na_n)}{ a_0} \\ \frac{ (b_0a_0 + b_1a_1 + ... + b_na_n)}{ a_1} \\ \vdots \\ \frac{ (b_0a_0 + b_1a_1 + ... + b_na_n)}{ a_n} \\ \end{bmatrix} =\begin{bmatrix} b_0 \\ b_1 \\ \vdots \\ b_n \end{bmatrix} = \mathbf{b}$$

Then show that: $$\frac{ \mathbf{a^T}\mathbf{A}\mathbf{a} }{ \mathbf{a}} = \mathbf{a}^T (\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{A}^T)$$

$$\mathbf{a}^T\mathbf{A}\mathbf{a} = [a_0,a_1, ... , a_n] \begin{bmatrix} A_{00} & A_{01} & ... & A_{0n}\\ A_{10} & A_{11} & ... & A_{1n}\\ \vdots & \vdots & \vdots & \vdots\\ A_{n0} & A_{n1} & ... & A_{nn}\\ \end{bmatrix} \begin{bmatrix} a_0 \\ a_1 \\ \vdots \\ a_n \end{bmatrix}$$ $$ = [a_0,a_1, ... , a_n] \begin{bmatrix} \sum_{i} A_{0i}a_i\\ \sum_{i} A_{1i}a_i\\ \vdots \\ \sum_{i} A_{ni}a_i\\ \end{bmatrix} = \sum_{ij}\left(a_jA_{ji}a_i \right) $$ $$\frac{ }{ a_k} \sum_{ij}\left(a_jA_{ji}a_i \right) = _{j}A_{kj}a_j + _{i}A{ki}a_i = \mathbf{a}^T(\mathbf{A} + \mathbf{A}^T)$$

Lastly, show that: $$\frac{ (\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{As})^T(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})}{ \mathbf{s}} = -2(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{A}$$

To show that $$\frac{ (\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{As})^T(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})}{ \mathbf{s}} = -2(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{A}$$

Let's start by expanding the expression on the left-hand side:

$$\frac{ (\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{As})^T(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})}{ \mathbf{s}} = \frac{ (\mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{As} - (\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{x} + (\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{As})}{ \mathbf{s}}$$

  1. $\frac{ \mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{x}}{ \mathbf{s}} = 0$ since $\mathbf{x}$ is not a function of $\mathbf{s}$.

  2. $\mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{As} = (\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{x}$, since both are scalars, so $$\frac{ - \mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{As} - (\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{x}}{ \mathbf{s}} = -2 \frac{ \mathbf{x}^T\mathbf{As}}{\mathbf{s}} = -2\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{x}$$

  3. $$\frac{ (\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{As}}{ \mathbf{s}} = \frac{ \mathbf{s}^T\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{As}}{ \mathbf{s}} = \sum_j \frac{ s_j}{ s_k}\sum_i (A^TA)_{ij}s_i + \sum_j s_j \sum_i (A^TA)_{ij}\frac{ s_i}{ s_k}$$ Since $(A^TA)$ is symmetric, we can combine the two sums: $$\frac{ (\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{As}}{ \mathbf{s}} = 2\sum_i (A^TA)_{ki}s_i = 2(A^TA)_{k:}\mathbf{s} = 2\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{As}$$

  4. Now we can put it all together:

$$\frac{ (\mathbf{x} - \mathbf{As})^T(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})}{ \mathbf{s}} = -2\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{x} + 2\mathbf{A}^T\mathbf{As} = -2(\mathbf{x}-\mathbf{As})^T\mathbf{A}$$
